# Science Magazine — Research Article Guidelines

Source: https://www.science.org/content/page/science-information-authors (accessed 2026-04-29)

## Standard Research Article
- **Main text:** ≤3,000 words (excluding abstract, legends, refs, acknowledgments)
- **Display items:** 3–5 figures or tables, with brief legends
- **References:** ~50 main-text references
- **Structure:** Abstract + main text divided into sections with brief subheadings + structured acknowledgments
- **Materials and Methods:** In supplementary materials (not main text), sufficient for replication
- **Data:** Must be in main text, supplementary, or deposited publicly

## Digital-Only Format
- Articles not suited for print (often >4 figures) go in digital editions
- Print gets a ~500-word summary (Introduction, Rationale, Results, Conclusion) + 1 summary figure (≤65-word caption)
- Methods run after main text as part of article; other supplementary remains separate

## Extended Format (editor-approved)
- **Main text:** up to 6,000 words
- **Display items:** up to 6 (may include videos)
- **References:** up to 100 main-text
- Cover letter must justify additional length
- For meta-analyses with >250 total refs, contact editors before submission
